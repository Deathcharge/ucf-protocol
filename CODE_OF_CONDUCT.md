## Our Commitment

The UCF Protocol community is dedicated to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all participants. We are committed to fostering a culture of collaboration, innovation, and mutual respect.

We believe that:

- **Diversity strengthens our community** - We welcome people of all backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives
- **Respect is fundamental** - We treat all community members with dignity and courtesy
- **Collaboration drives progress** - We work together toward shared goals
- **Transparency builds trust** - We communicate openly and honestly
- **Ethics guide our actions** - We uphold ethical principles in all interactions

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community members, contributors, and maintainers
- All project spaces (GitHub, Discord, forums, events)
- Official project communications
- Interactions that reference or represent the project

This Code of Conduct applies equally to all participants, regardless of:

- Position or status
- Experience level
- Background or identity
- Affiliation or organization

---

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

### 1. Be Respectful

- Treat all people with respect and dignity
- Listen actively to others' perspectives
- Acknowledge and value different viewpoints
- Avoid dismissive or condescending language
- Respect personal boundaries and preferences

### 2. Be Inclusive

- Welcome people of all backgrounds and experiences
- Make efforts to include underrepresented voices
- Avoid assumptions about others' identities or experiences
- Use inclusive language and terminology
- Create space for diverse perspectives

### 3. Be Collaborative

- Work together toward shared goals
- Support and help other community members
- Share knowledge and expertise generously
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate others' contributions and achievements

### 4. Be Honest

- Communicate openly and transparently
- Acknowledge mistakes and learn from them
- Provide accurate information
- Admit when you don't know something
- Follow through on commitments

### 5. Be Ethical

- Respect intellectual property rights
- Maintain confidentiality of private information
- Follow applicable laws and regulations
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Report concerns through appropriate channels

### 6. Be Constructive

- Provide feedback that helps others improve
- Focus on ideas, not personal attacks
- Assume good intent in others' actions
- Offer solutions, not just criticism
- Help resolve conflicts peacefully

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in the UCF Protocol community:

### 1. Harassment and Discrimination

- Harassment based on protected characteristics (race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, etc.)
- Unwelcome sexual advances or comments
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Stalking or doxxing (sharing private information)

### 2. Hate Speech and Slurs

- Slurs or derogatory language targeting individuals or groups
- Promotion of discrimination or violence
- Hateful ideologies or conspiracy theories
- Dehumanizing language

### 3. Personal Attacks

- Insults or name-calling
- Ad hominem attacks
- Mockery or ridicule
- Deliberate humiliation
- Passive-aggressive behavior

### 4. Trolling and Disruption

- Intentional disruption of discussions
- Spam or off-topic content
- Deliberate provocation
- Spreading misinformation
- Coordinated attacks

### 5. Abuse of Power

- Misuse of moderator or maintainer privileges
- Retaliation against those who report violations
- Favoritism or nepotism
- Abuse of trust or authority

### 6. Privacy Violations

- Sharing private information without consent
- Recording or photographing without permission
- Doxxing or identifying individuals
- Breaching confidentiality agreements

### 7. Illegal Activity

- Harassment or threats
- Fraud or deception
- Intellectual property violations
- Data theft or unauthorized access
- Other illegal conduct

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Email**: conduct@ucf-protocol.dev  
**Discord**: DM @Moderator  
**GitHub**: Private message to maintainers  
**Anonymous**: [Anonymous reporting form - link]

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

1. **Description**: What happened? Be specific and factual
2. **People involved**: Who was involved? (names or usernames)
3. **Date/Time**: When did this occur?
4. **Location**: Where did this happen? (GitHub issue, Discord, etc.)
5. **Evidence**: Screenshots, links, or other documentation
6. **Impact**: How did this affect you or others?
7. **Witnesses**: Were there other people present?

### Confidentiality

- Reports are handled confidentially
- Your identity will be protected unless you request otherwise
- We will not disclose reporter information without consent
- Investigation details are shared only with those who need to know

### No Retaliation

We prohibit retaliation against anyone who reports violations in good faith. Retaliation is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement

### Investigation Process

1. **Receipt**: We acknowledge receipt of reports within 24 hours
2. **Assessment**: We assess the severity and urgency
3. **Investigation**: We gather information from all parties
4. **Determination**: We determine if a violation occurred
5. **Action**: We take appropriate corrective action
6. **Follow-up**: We follow up with affected parties

### Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity and context, enforcement actions may include:

| Violation | First Offense | Repeat Offense |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Minor disrespect | Warning | Temporary mute |
| Harassment | Temporary ban | Permanent ban |
| Hate speech | Temporary ban | Permanent ban |
| Threats/violence | Immediate ban | N/A |
| Illegal activity | Report to authorities | N/A |

### Escalation

- **Level 1**: Warning and education
- **Level 2**: Temporary restrictions (mute, ban from channels)
- **Level 3**: Temporary suspension (24-72 hours)
- **Level 4**: Extended suspension (1-4 weeks)
- **Level 5**: Permanent removal from community

---

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

Community members subject to enforcement actions have the right to appeal:

**Appeal Process**:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Email appeal@ucf-protocol.dev within 7 days
2. **Review**: Appeals committee reviews the case
3. **Hearing**: You may request a hearing to present your case
4. **Decision**: Committee makes final determination
5. **Notification**: You're notified of the outcome

**Appeal Deadline**: 7 days from enforcement action

### Appeals Committee

- Composed of 3-5 community members
- Includes diverse perspectives
- Excludes those directly involved
- Makes decisions by majority vote
- Provides written explanation of decision

## Special Considerations

### Mental Health and Crisis Support

If you're experiencing a mental health crisis:

- **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline**: 988 (US)
- **Crisis Text Line**: Text HOME to 741741
- **International Association for Suicide Prevention**: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/

### Accessibility

We are committed to accessibility:

- Use alt text for images
- Provide transcripts for videos
- Use clear, simple language
- Avoid flashing or seizure-inducing content
- Respect accessibility needs

### Neurodiversity

We recognize and respect neurodiversity:

- Accept different communication styles
- Provide accommodations when possible
- Avoid ableist language
- Be patient with different processing speeds
- Recognize different strengths and challenges

## Appendix: Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Harassment** | Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics |
| **Discrimination** | Unfair treatment based on identity or characteristics |
| **Hate speech** | Speech that attacks or demeans groups based on identity |
| **Doxxing** | Sharing private information to enable harassment |
| **Retaliation** | Negative action taken against someone for reporting violations |
| **Confidentiality** | Keeping information private and not sharing without consent |
| **Accessibility** | Ensuring content is usable by people with disabilities |
| **Neurodiversity** | Natural variation in neurological functioning |
| **Ableism** | Discrimination based on disability or neurological difference |
| **Inclusive language** | Language that doesn't exclude or demean groups |
